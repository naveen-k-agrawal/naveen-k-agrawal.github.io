Naveen Agrawal

Marie Skłodowska-Curie PhD Researcher · KTH Royal Institute of Technology

Engineering Mechanics · Rheology · CFD · Soft Matter · Biophysics

I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie PhD researcher at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, specializing in simulating dense particle suspensions and investigating the relationship between microscopic interactions and bulk fluid properties. My work is part of the YIELDGAP project (Horizon 2020), focusing on yield-stress fluids beyond Bingham models.

I hold an MS (Research) in Thermal Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My research interests span complex fluids, fluid mechanics, CFD, soft matter, and biophysics. I have conducted research visits at ESPCI Paris – PSL and Procter & Gamble (Brussels).

Projects

YIELDGAP — Dense Particle Suspensions

KTH · O. Tammisola, L. Brandt · 2021–Present

Simulating dense particle suspensions to investigate the link between microscopic particle interactions and bulk rheological properties of yield-stress fluids. Part of the EU Horizon 2020 Marie Skłodowska-Curie ITN.

Paper (JFM 2025)

Rheology of Colloidal Silica Gels

ESPCI Paris – PSL · 2022

Experimental investigation of scaling laws governing the rheological properties of colloidal silica gels using Ares G2 and Anton Paar rheometers.

Active-Passive Particle Systems

IIT Madras · PS Mahapatra · 2019–2021

Studied phase transitions and collective behavior including motility-induced segregation in mixtures of active and passive microswimmers using particle-based simulations.

PRE 2021 PRE 2020

MPCD Simulation of Janus Microswimmers

IIT Madras · PS Mahapatra · 2020–2021

Developed Multi-Particle Collision Dynamics (MPCD) algorithm to model slip-stick Janus particles in Newtonian fluids. Implemented in C/C++ with CUDA for GPU-accelerated parallel computing.
